Reflection

"

Media and Information Literacy has reshaped the way I approach everything I create online. Before this subject, I built things because they worked — now I build them with a sharper awareness of why they exist, who sees them, and what they communicate beyond their function.

Understanding the difference between information, misinformation, and media bias made me more deliberate as a developer. Every element on a webpage — a color, a label, a layout choice — is a form of communication. This portfolio itself is a media product, and MIL taught me to treat it like one.

The activities we completed, from analyzing media sources to producing our own content, showed me that digital literacy isn't just about consuming media critically — it's about being a responsible creator too. Building Sand Box 3D and managing HEXON Laboratories means I'm putting content out into the world, and I now carry the responsibility that comes with that.

This subject didn't just teach me to fact-check. It taught me to think about access, representation, and the real weight of what we publish — lessons I'll carry well past this class.

Media Literacy Critical Thinking Responsible Creation Digital Citizenship
